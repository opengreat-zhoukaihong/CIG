the Breakthrough of Online Tourism Industry will undertake three years

Hongkong Economic Daily Reporter Linruiyi
....The three largest travel agencies in China, namely China International Travel Agency Headquarter, China Travel Agency and China Youth Travel Agency, are combining the traditional business pattern and online travel business, however, some people in the industry hold the view that the online travel business will still depend a lot on telephone network.
....CEO of CNbooking.com, Feng Muk Ching, remarks that the present domestic tourism industry has not take full use of Internet to develop businesses, that is mainly because the course of booking service requires a lot of timely and mutual communication, While at present, Internet can not accomplish these perfectly, therefore, telephone booking will still be used prior to Internet in the next three years.
....Feng added, now it is limited by the environment to run travel website in Mainland. The domestic network information technology is not stable and the use of credits is not popular in mainland, all these will narrow the development of online travel booking business. But he believes that online booking will parallel with telephone booking in several years and by and by will take the place of telephone commerce.
....Feng said, Meilin Corp is a successful story of running E-commerce website whose proportion of telephone booking turnover to online booking is 9 to 1. This shows the great vitality of telephone commerce. And the situation of CNHotelbooking.com is much like this.
....CNHotel Booking Center mainly offers the service of booking hotels above 3-star standard in Mainland China, Hong Kong and Macao with a list of more than 1000 hotels. It owns offline companies respectively set in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Dalian and Hong Kong to deal with telephone consultation and meet the need of business.
....Apart from hotel booking, the Center also offers outfit services, such as renting office equipment, arranging entertainment, and temporary interpreter, secretary and tour guide.
....At present, travel websites in Mainland China can be classified into two groups, one of which consists of the traditional travel agencies developing E-commerce and the other is made up of sheer travel website offering comprehensive services of complete information of itineraries, guide of tourist spots, ticket booking and hotel booking, and so on.

(Extract from Hong Kong Economy Daily of July 18,2000)
