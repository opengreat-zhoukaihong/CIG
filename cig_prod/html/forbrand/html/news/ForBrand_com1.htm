ForBrand Introduces Unique Benchmarks for Provider Profiling Software


CAMBRIDGE, Mass.--(BW HealthWire)--Oct. 26, 2000--iMcKesson LLC, a subsidiary of McKesson HBOC, Inc., today announced the addition of clinically comprehensive regional and national benchmarks to its provider profiling software, Episode Profiler(R), which improve the ability of healthcare plans to identify medical management opportunities. Episode Profiler, part of iMcKesson's Clinical Resource Management System (CRMS(TM)), helps payors realize cost and quality improvements through detailed information on how physicians treat patients with similar conditions, which services patients receive and how much treatment costs.

Differences in treatment are analyzed using episodes of care, a consistent basis of comparison that takes into account all of the care a patient receives for an illness, including medical, surgical and pharmaceutical interventions. iMcKesson created the normative data in collaboration with the healthcare information firm PharMetrics, Inc., leveraging the PharMetrics Integrated Outcomes Database of over 16 million lives of encrypted, medical and pharmaceutical data. The statistically significant and geographically dispersed normative data is uniquely tailored for use with Episode Profiler, applying the same analytic methods used in the CRMS suite of products. Both CRMS and the Integrated Outcomes Database employ Episode Treatment Groups(TM) (ETG), the leading approach to defining clinical episodes developed by Symmetry Health Data Systems, Inc.(R).

"Users of Episode Profiler can now produce reports that compare the performance of their providers to peers on a regional and national level in addition to those in their own network, said Stanley Hochberg, MD, a vice president for product management, iMcKesson Medical Management. "And because these reports match the metrics and screen format found in Episode Profiler, they provide apples-to-apples comparisons." The CRMS normative data is unique in the detail displayed at each level of reporting, which corresponds to the detail already contained in Episode Profiler. Payors can view a broad range of customized cost and utilization measures at the broadest level of major practice categories, such as cardiology, down to ETGs and then to individual procedure and pharmacy codes used in treating a particular type of episode, such as a heart attack. A second version of the reports to be delivered this winter will provide yet another level of "drill down" information describing provider performance benchmarks at the specialty level. "The level and type of information offered by the new software will be of great benefit in helping payors to identify key areas for improving the quality of care while managing costs," said Hochberg. "For example, if an MCO is experiencing unusually high pharmacy costs, Episode Profiler will now not only identify the specific illnesses and related prescriptions responsible for those high costs, but also provide a context for whether those costs are high relative to the combined experience of health plans or geographic areas."
